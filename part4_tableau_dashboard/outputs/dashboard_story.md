# Dashboard Story
**For**: Leadership Team  
**Subject**: What our sales data is telling us — and what to do about it

---

## Executive Summary

Our business is generating strong total sales (₹216.8M) but the story underneath the numbers reveals three things leadership needs to act on:

1. **One category (Technology) is carrying almost all the profit** — Furniture and Office Supplies together contribute less than 16% of total profit
2. **Discounts above 20% are loss-making** — we are currently running promotions that cost us money on every order
3. **The South region significantly outperforms East and West** — and we do not yet fully understand why

The good news: no single customer segment dominates, organic traffic is thriving, and Same Day shipping correlates with our highest-value orders. The business has a solid foundation — it just needs sharper guardrails on discounting and a more balanced category strategy.

---

## What Is Performing Well

### Technology Is Our Profit Engine
Technology generates ₹28M in profit — 84% of our total profit from just one of three categories. Margins are strong, return rates are low (3%), and customers buying Technology tend to place higher-value orders. This is the category to protect and grow.

### South Region Is Our Star Market
South region consistently outperforms all others — ₹64.7M in sales and ₹10M in profit. Whatever is working in South (store positioning, local marketing, product mix) is worth studying and replicating elsewhere.

### Organic Traffic Delivers Our Best Customers
₹88.8M of sales come from Organic traffic — more than Paid, Email, Social, and Referral combined. This suggests strong brand recognition and customer trust. It is our most cost-efficient acquisition channel.

### Revenue Is Growing Year-Over-Year
Monthly sales in H2 2025 averaged above ₹9.5M — stronger than H1 2025 and showing the business is on an upward trajectory overall.

---

## What Is Underperforming

### Furniture Is Costly and Returned Frequently
Furniture contributes only ₹3.6M in profit on ₹51.6M in sales — a profit margin of just 6.9%. Worse, it has the highest return rate at 7.67%. Every returned Furniture item costs money in reverse logistics, restocking, and potential damage. Furniture is consuming operational resources without delivering proportionate profit.

### East and West Regions Are 25% Behind South
East (₹48.9M) and West (₹48.9M) regions are performing similarly to each other but significantly below South (₹64.7M). Without understanding the root cause, we cannot close this gap. It may be market size, store quality, product mix, or customer acquisition efficiency.

### Q2 2025 Had a Clear Revenue Dip
April and June 2025 were our two weakest months (₹7.2M and ₹7.3M). This dip happened during a period when marketing campaigns may have paused or seasonal demand was lower. It is a gap in our annual revenue rhythm that should be planned for.

---

## What Risks Are Visible

### Heavy Discounting Is Loss-Making
Orders with discounts above 30% generate negative average profit (–₹1,601 per order). This means the business is effectively subsidising those customers. If a significant portion of volume is driven by deep discounts, we are growing sales at the cost of profit.

### Over-Reliance on One Category
84% of profit coming from Technology is a concentration risk. A supply chain disruption, a competitor price drop, or a demand shift in Technology could significantly damage our overall profitability with very little cushion from other categories.

### Home Office Return Rate Is Elevated
Home Office customers return products at 6% — 50% higher than Consumer and Corporate segments. If this continues, it will increase reverse logistics costs and reduce net revenue from this segment.

---

## What Opportunities Are Visible

### Grow Technology Further
With strong margins and low return rates, Technology is underserving its potential. Adding more SKUs, improving product discovery, and offering bundling could increase both basket size and order frequency.

### Fix Furniture or Deprioritise It
Either improve Furniture margins through supplier renegotiation and reduce returns through better product descriptions — or deprioritise shelf space and marketing investment in Furniture and redirect it to Technology.

### Replicate South Region Success
Study the South region in detail — product mix, pricing strategy, customer acquisition channels, store formats. Then build a specific plan to replicate those conditions in East and West.

### Cap Discounts at 20%
Implementing a hard cap on standard discounts at 20% would immediately protect profit margins on every high-discount order. This single policy change could add millions to annual profit without reducing revenue volume significantly.

---

## Recommended Business Actions (Priority Order)

1. **Immediate**: Implement 20% discount cap policy for standard orders
2. **Short-term**: Conduct a South region audit to identify replicable success factors for East and West
3. **Short-term**: Review top Furniture return sub-categories and improve product descriptions
4. **Medium-term**: Build a Technology category growth plan — more SKUs, bundles, loyalty pricing
5. **Ongoing**: Monitor Home Office return rate monthly — investigate if it remains above 5%

---

## Limitations of the Dashboard

1. The data covers 2024–2025 only — longer historical trends are not visible
2. Cost of customer acquisition per channel is not in the dataset — Organic may look best on revenue but we cannot confirm ROI without knowing acquisition cost
3. Customer-level data is order-level — repeat purchase rates and customer lifetime value cannot be calculated from this data alone
4. 32 missing customer_rating values and 24 missing campaign_channel values — a small portion of records is incomplete

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer LTV analysis** — link customer_id across orders to understand repeat purchase behaviour
2. **Discount elasticity study** — test whether reducing discounts from 30% to 20% actually reduces order volume or just improves margins
3. **Regional deep-dive** — build a store-level analysis for East and West to identify which specific locations are dragging performance
4. **Return root cause analysis** — survey returned Furniture customers to understand why they returned
